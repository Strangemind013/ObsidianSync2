
Since Gida was up to his ears in debt, he settled for merely gambling with his life. Wasn't much of a life, to be honest, but it was all he had to work with. 

He usually taught the lessons at the garden, and so there he waited. One of the stewards served his usual tea and a platter of assorted nuts and sunflower seeds. Some quality-looking lacquerware they had here. He thought about nicking one of the teacups on the way out, as he was nearing the end of his tenure anyway. Straight-backed and with all the grace and dignity he could muster, he sipped as much green tea as etiquette would allow. He didn't think he'd be so fond of the stuff. *Bitter leaf juice*, he once called it. Funny how threats to one's life opened up an appreciation for things otherwise ignored.
