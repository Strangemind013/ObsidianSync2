Writing in the Style of Joe Abercrombie

# 1. Blending Cunning and Compassion: Joe Abercrombie’s Style

  
Abercrombie’s characters, particularly those who blend cunning with compassion, stand out due to their layered voices. The key to this is a mix of sharp intellect, keen observation, and a kind but unsentimental heart. Let’s break it down:  
  
1. **Sharp, Precise Inner Monologue:**  
   A cunning character’s thoughts are often one step ahead. They’re always calculating, assessing people and situations with laser precision.  
    
   - **Normal sentence**: “He wasn’t telling the truth.”  
   - **Abercrombie-style**: “He lied, of course. But the truth could only be somewhere close by.”  
    
   Abercrombie’s characters, like Monza Murcatto or Glokta, are always suspicious. They don’t trust easily, but they keep it to themselves. Instead of being openly skeptical, they observe quietly, waiting for the right moment.  
    
2. **Understated Compassion:**  
   While they’re calculating, Abercrombie’s characters aren’t heartless. There’s always a layer of empathy, but it’s not overt. For instance, a character might show care through practical means, rather than soft words or gestures.  
    
   - **Normal sentence**: “I’m worried about you.”  
   - **Abercrombie-style**: “I’d keep your head down if I were you. Would hate to see it roll.”  
    
   Here, the concern is hidden under pragmatism. The character doesn’t express affection directly, but the protective advice reveals that they care.  
  
---  
  
### 2. Structuring a Clever, Observant, Yet Kind Voice  
To create a voice that is both cunning and compassionate, balance is key. Abercrombie does this by using contradiction in his characters. They think one way but act another, they hide their true feelings behind sarcasm, but still care deeply.  
  
### Example Rewrite:  
- **Normal sentence**: “We can win this.”  
- **Abercrombie-style**: "Win? We’re all dead men walking. I’ve seen barn mice with better odds. But there’s always a chance, however small. I say we take our shot.”  
    
The outward words show confidence, but the inner thoughts betray doubt. The character doesn’t need to speak every truth—part of being cunning is choosing which truths to show and which to hide.  
  
---  
  

# 3. Clever Turns of Phrase

  
Abercrombie is known for his clever turns of phrase, particularly for characters who are both intelligent and world-weary. These characters often express complex thoughts through sharp, memorable lines.  
  
### Example Rewrite:  
- **Normal**: “You’re making a mistake.”  
- **Abercrombie-style**: “A mistake? Could be. I’ve made plenty. But this one feels... familiar, doesn’t it? Last time it didn’t end so well, though. Still, your call, of course.”  
  
In this rewrite, the character subtly questions the decision, pointing to their experience without directly challenging the authority of the person they’re speaking to. It’s indirect, but effective.  
  
---  
  
### 4. Blending Humor and Dark Realism  
Humor, particularly dark humor, is essential to Abercrombie’s style. His characters often use humor as a defense mechanism or a way to cope with grim situations.  
  
### Example Rewrite:  
- **Normal**: “I don’t know if we’ll make it.”  
- **Abercrombie-style**: “Make it? I’d be surprised if we don’t end up face-first in a ditch by nightfall. But hey, I’ve been wrong before. Once or twice.”  
  
By adding humor and self-deprecation, the character conveys the seriousness of the situation without being overly dramatic. They acknowledge the danger but keep it light-hearted.  
  
---  
  

# 5. Subtle Manipulation: Flipping Power Dynamics

  
In Abercrombie’s world, cunning characters know how to navigate conversations without outright confrontation. They manipulate situations through carefully chosen words.  
  
### Example Rewrite:  
- **Normal**: “You’re wrong about this.”  
- **Abercrombie-style**: “I hate to admit it, but I’m not sure. Could be the right move, could be the one that gets us killed. This would come down to a gut feeling, and mine is not to be trusted. Let’s go with yours.”  
  
In this case, the character concedes the possibility of being wrong, but still manages to cast doubt on the other person’s decision. It’s a soft challenge, one that invites reconsideration without force.

### 6. Writing in Logen Ninefingers’ Style  
Logen Ninefingers’ voice is distinct because of his pragmatic, resigned tone. He’s been through too much to be an idealist, but he’s not entirely hopeless. There’s always a sense of grim determination, tinged with a bit of fatalism.  
  
### Example Rewrite:  
- **Normal**: “This is dangerous.”  
- **Logen Ninefingers-style**: “Dangerous? Aye, could be. But what isn’t? You want to live, you’ve got to stand up to it. Or lie down and let it trample you.”  
  
Logen’s perspective always acknowledges the harshness of the world, but his response is simple: you deal with it or you die.

# 7. Blending Compassion with Cunning: Further Exploration

  
The magic of blending a cunning mind with a compassionate heart lies in the tension between the two. A character can be calculating, but if their motives are rooted in care for others, it makes them far more compelling. Abercrombie achieves this through:  
  
- **Brutal honesty wrapped in understanding**: Characters tell hard truths, but there’s a sense that they’re doing it for the other person’s benefit.  
- **Dark humor as a shield**: Humor deflects pain, but also keeps others at a distance.  
- **Observation as a form of empathy**: Keen observation reveals not just sharpness but a deep understanding of human nature.  
  
### Example Rewrite:  
- **Normal**: “You’re taking a risk.”  
- **Abercrombie-style**: “You’re playing with fire. But if you’re lucky, you’ll get a good burn out of it. And if you’re smart, you’ll know when to quit before it eats you alive.”  
  
This blend of sharp observation, dark humor, and concern creates a voice that is both cunning and compassionate, the perfect balance for a character like Monza Murcatto or a similar figure in your own writing.
